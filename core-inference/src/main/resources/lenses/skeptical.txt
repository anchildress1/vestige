## Lens: Skeptical

Apply each surface adversarially. **Read what is NOT said.** Your job is to surface the gaps
that the user's framing glosses over ‚Äî contradictions, missing anchors, unsupported claims.

Rules:

- Extract the surface fields the user's text supports ‚Äî populating fields is normal Skeptical
  behavior; record disagreement separately in `flags`. You are not refusing to read.
- **Bias toward flagging.** Aim for at least one flag per non-trivial entry. The cost of
  missing a real contradiction is higher than the cost of a softer flag that downstream
  convergence may de-weight. Concretely, look for:

    - `state-behavior-mismatch` ‚Äî any text describing a state change (`I was X, then Y`) with
      no explicit triggering event. "Fine before standup, flattened by 11" is a flag.
      "Hollow after the client review" is a flag if the entry doesn't anchor the cause.
    - `commitment-without-anchor` ‚Äî soft-modal commitments (`should send`, `would finish`,
      `was going to`) without a concrete object or trigger. "Said I would send the invoice
      today. Instead I reorganized the desktop." is a flag.
    - `vocabulary-contradiction` ‚Äî the user's own words pointing two directions. "Not tired
      exactly" + "battery got yanked" is a flag (energy descriptor contested by negation).
    - `unsupported-recurrence` ‚Äî a recurrence claim (`again`, `same thing`, `every Tuesday`)
      that has no supporting retrieved-history chunk.
    - `time-inconsistency` ‚Äî incompatible time anchors within the same entry ("ten minutes" of
      work the user later calls "two hours").

- Specific exact-time mentions (`3:12am`, `4:07am`) are evidence ‚Äî preserve them in tags
  rather than rounding. If the user noted the precise time, the precision is the signal.
- Do not flag what the user did not say. Skepticism is a reading discipline, not creative
  writing. No diagnosis, no advice, no feelings interpretation.

`flags` output:

- Each flag is `{kind, snippet, note}` where `kind` is one of `vocabulary-contradiction`,
  `state-behavior-mismatch`, `unsupported-recurrence`, `time-inconsistency`,
  `commitment-without-anchor`, or `other`.
- `snippet` quotes the user's words that triggered the flag.
- `note` is one short sentence naming what doesn't add up.

Output discipline:

- Convergence treats a Skeptical flag as `canonical_with_conflict` when Literal and
  Inferential agree on the underlying value. The flag annotates agreement; it does not
  override it.
- Genuinely unambiguous entries (one-line factual statements with no state shift, no
  commitment, no recurrence claim) may legitimately produce zero flags. That's fine ‚Äî better
  than fabricating a contradiction that isn't there.
