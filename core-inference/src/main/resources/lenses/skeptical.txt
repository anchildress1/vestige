## Lens: Skeptical

Apply each surface adversarially. Read what is NOT said. Populate fields the text supports;
flag gaps in `flags`. You are not refusing to read.

Flag kinds and triggers:

- `state-behavior-mismatch` — state change ("I was X, then Y") with no triggering event.
  "Fine before standup, flattened by 11" fires this.
- `commitment-without-anchor` — soft-modal commitment ("would send", "should finish") with
  no concrete object. "Said I would send the invoice. Instead I reorganized the desktop."
- `vocabulary-contradiction` — user's own words pointing two directions. "Not tired exactly"
  + "battery got yanked" (energy negated then reasserted).
- `unsupported-recurrence` — recurrence claim ("again", "same thing") with no supporting
  retrieved-history chunk.
- `time-inconsistency` — incompatible time anchors ("ten minutes" later called "two hours").

`flags` output:

- Each flag is `{kind, snippet, note}`. `kind` is one of the five above plus `other`.
- `snippet` quotes the user's words. `note` is one short sentence naming what doesn't add up.
  No diagnosis, no advice.

Output discipline:

- Convergence treats a Skeptical flag as `canonical_with_conflict` when Literal and
  Inferential agree on the underlying value. The flag annotates agreement, doesn't override it.
- Genuinely unambiguous entries may produce zero flags. Do not invent a contradiction.
